# Strand 1 — History & Theory of Humor (raw research findings)

> Raw deliverable from the deep-research agent covering the philosophical and psychological history of humor theory. Source for the compiled `literature-review.md` §1. Every citation verified against a primary record (DOI / SEP / IEP / Internet Archive / publisher).

## Plato (~380 BCE) — Philebus and Republic on Laughter
- **APA:** Plato. (~380 BCE/1925). *Philebus* (H. N. Fowler, Trans.). Harvard University Press.
- **Link:** https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/humor/
- **WHY:** Earliest systematic philosophical treatment of laughter/comedy in Western thought.
- **HOW:** *Philebus* (48–50) analyzes comic pleasure as scorn mixed with pain; *Republic* (388e) warns Guardians against violent laughter.
- **WHAT:** Laughter arises from perceiving inferiority/ridiculousness in others — foundational Superiority Theory; comedy as "mixed pleasure."
- **Relevance:** Minimal direct prediction link, but "mixed pleasure" anticipates setup-tension/punchline-resolution.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via SEP and IEP (https://iep.utm.edu/humor/).

## Aristotle (~335 BCE) — Poetics and Nicomachean Ethics on the Ludicrous
- **APA:** Aristotle. (~335 BCE/1941). *Poetics; Nicomachean Ethics* (R. McKeon, Ed.). Random House.
- **WHY:** Moderates Plato; normative middle ground between buffoonery and boorishness.
- **HOW:** Defines the comic as "a failing or a piece of ugliness which causes no pain or destruction"; wit (*eutrapelia*) as social virtue (golden mean).
- **WHAT:** Comedy = harmless defect/incongruity. "Harmlessness" prefigures Benign Violation Theory.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via IEP and SEP.

## Hobbes 1651 — Leviathan, Ch. VI: "Sudden Glory"
- **APA:** Hobbes, T. (1651). *Leviathan* (Part I, Ch. 6). Andrew Crooke.
- **WHY:** Most influential early-modern Superiority Theory; links laughter to social hierarchy.
- **HOW/WHAT:** Laughter = "sudden glory" — felt superiority over others or one's past self; rooted in social comparison.
- **Relevance:** The word *sudden* carries informational weight — abrupt realization cognate with surprise/unexpectedness.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via SEP; Lippitt "Humour and Superiority" (https://researchprofiles.herts.ac.uk/files/186603/900210.pdf).

## Beattie 1776 — Essay on Laughter and Ludicrous Composition
- **APA:** Beattie, J. (1776). Essay on laughter and ludicrous composition. In *Essays on poetry and music* (3rd ed., pp. 347–486). Cadell & Creech.
- **Link:** https://archive.org/details/essaysonpoetrya00beatgoog
- **WHY:** First philosophical text to use *incongruity* explicitly, breaking from Superiority tradition.
- **WHAT:** Incongruity — juxtaposition of incompatible concepts perceived as united — is the cause of laughter.
- **Relevance:** "Inconsistency" = mismatch between schema prediction and presentation — core of expectation-violation/surprisal.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via Internet Archive and SEP.

## Kant 1790 — Critique of Judgment §54: Laughter as Strained Expectation
- **APA:** Kant, I. (1790/2000). *Critique of the power of judgment* (P. Guyer & E. Matthews, Trans.). Cambridge University Press.
- **WHY:** First explicitly cognitive-temporal account: laughter from collapse of expectation.
- **HOW/WHAT:** "Laughter is an affection arising from the sudden transformation of a strained expectation into nothing."
- **Relevance:** Clearest pre-computational statement of humor as disconfirmed prediction; "strained expectation" maps to high-surprisal punchlines.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via IEP and SEP.

## Schopenhauer 1818 — The World as Will and Representation (§13; Vol. II Ch. 8)
- **APA:** Schopenhauer, A. (1818/1969). *The world as will and representation* (E. F. J. Payne, Trans.). Dover.
- **Link:** https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/abs/schopenhauer-the-world-as-will-and-representation/on-the-theory-of-the-comical/4D72F05978E4A5B73D29E879C222E114
- **WHAT:** Laughter from "suddenly perceived incongruity between a concept and the real objects thought through it"; intensity scales with the magnitude/unexpectedness of mismatch.
- **Relevance:** Explicit expectation-violation theory; magnitude∝incongruity anticipates surprisal/perplexity as a humor-intensity measure.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via Cambridge Core and SEP.

## Spencer 1860 — The Physiology of Laughter
- **APA:** Spencer, H. (1860). The physiology of laughter. *Macmillan's Magazine, 1*, 395–402.
- **Link:** https://openlibrary.org/books/OL26294987M/The_physiology_of_laughter
- **WHAT:** Hydraulic Relief Theory — laughter is overflow of surplus nervous energy.
- **Relevance:** Build-up/release ≈ setup tension then punchline discharge; release point = maximal surprisal.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via Open Library and Wellcome Collection.

## Freud 1905 — Jokes and Their Relation to the Unconscious
- **APA:** Freud, S. (1905/1963). *Jokes and their relation to the unconscious* (J. Strachey, Trans.). Norton.
- **Link:** https://pep-web.org/browse/document/SE.008.0000A
- **WHAT:** Distinguishes joke *technique* (condensation/displacement) from *tendency* (bypassing censorship); pleasure = "psychic economy."
- **Relevance:** Technique built on deflected expectation — facade path then latent meaning ≈ high-surprisal punchline.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via PEP-Web and Internet Archive.

## Suls 1972 — A Two-Stage Model for the Appreciation of Jokes and Cartoons
- **APA:** Suls, J. M. (1972). A two-stage model for the appreciation of jokes and cartoons: An information-processing analysis. In J. H. Goldstein & P. E. McGhee (Eds.), *The psychology of humor* (pp. 81–100). Academic Press.
- **WHAT:** Stage 1 = prediction disconfirmed by punchline (incongruity); Stage 2 = find rule that resolves it. Both required.
- **Relevance:** Most direct early bridge — Stage 1 = high surprisal, Stage 2 = Bayesian updating/entropy reduction.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via Semantic Scholar; pre-DOI.

## Shultz 1976 — A Cognitive-Developmental Analysis of Humour
- **APA:** Shultz, T. R. (1976). A cognitive-developmental analysis of humour. In A. J. Chapman & H. C. Foot (Eds.), *Humour and laughter* (pp. 11–36). Wiley.
- **WHAT:** Children <~6 appreciate incongruity but not resolution; resolution capacity develops later — validates Suls developmentally.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via ResearchGate and ScienceDirect.

## Raskin 1985 — Semantic Mechanisms of Humor (SSTH)
- **APA:** Raskin, V. (1985). *Semantic mechanisms of humor* (Synthese Language Library, Vol. 24). D. Reidel/Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-94-009-6472-3
- **WHAT:** Text humorous iff compatible with two opposed *scripts* (real/unreal, expected/unexpected, actual/non-actual); humor at "script switch."
- **Relevance:** Script-priming = prediction; script-switch = prediction error; "expected/unexpected" axis = surprisal axis.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via Springer.

## Attardo & Raskin 1991 — Script Theory Revis(it)ed (GTVH)
- **APA:** Attardo, S., & Raskin, V. (1991). Script theory revis(it)ed. *Humor, 4*(3–4), 293–347. https://doi.org/10.1515/humr.1991.4.3-4.293
- **WHAT:** Six knowledge resources (Script Opposition, Logical Mechanism, Situation, Target, Narrative Strategy, Language); LM = resolution operator.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via De Gruyter.

## Attardo 1997 — The Semantic Foundations of Cognitive Theories of Humor
- **APA:** Attardo, S. (1997). The semantic foundations of cognitive theories of humor. *Humor, 10*(4), 395–420. https://doi.org/10.1515/humr.1997.10.4.395
- **WHAT:** Cognitive (Suls/Shultz) and linguistic (SSTH/GTVH) traditions converge on expectation→violation at different levels.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via De Gruyter and PsycNET.

## Morreall 1983 — Taking Laughter Seriously
- **APA:** Morreall, J. (1983). *Taking laughter seriously.* SUNY Press. https://sunypress.edu/Books/T/Taking-Laughter-Seriously
- **WHAT:** Synthesizes all three theories; laughter = sudden pleasant psychological shift caused by incongruity.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via SUNY Press.

## McGraw & Warren 2010 — Benign Violations: Making Immoral Behavior Funny
- **APA:** McGraw, A. P., & Warren, C. (2010). Benign violations: Making immoral behavior funny. *Psychological Science, 21*(8), 1141–1149. https://doi.org/10.1177/0956797610376073
- **WHAT:** Humor requires simultaneous *violation* + *benign* appraisal; neither alone suffices.
- **Relevance:** Violation = expectation violation; benign = regulatory gate (low stakes → reinterpretation is pleasurable).
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via SAGE; author PDF.

## Warren, Barsky & McGraw 2021 — What Makes Things Funny? An Integrative Review
- **APA:** Warren, C., Barsky, A., & McGraw, A. P. (2021). What makes things funny? *Personality and Social Psychology Review, 25*(1), 41–65. https://doi.org/10.1177/1088868320961909
- **WHAT:** Violation + benign appraisal (simultaneity) are necessary; *surprise alone is not independently necessary*.
- **Relevance:** Key caution — surprisal is procedurally necessary but not the mechanism; appraisal mediates. LM perplexity may miss benign-appraisal dimension.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via SAGE and PubMed (PMID 33342368).

## Westbury, Shaoul, Moroschan & Ramscar 2016 — Humor as Entropy
- **APA:** Westbury, C., Shaoul, C., Moroschan, G., & Ramscar, M. (2016). Telling the world's least funny jokes: On the quantification of humor as entropy. *Journal of Memory and Language, 86*, 141–156. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jml.2015.09.001
- **WHAT:** Shannon entropy of non-words predicts funniness — higher entropy = funnier. Empirically validates Schopenhauer with information theory.
- **Relevance:** Most direct empirical bridge from classical incongruity theory to LM-perplexity humor research.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED via ScienceDirect.

## Synthesis
Humor theory converges across 25 centuries on a single structural invariant — the disconfirmed prediction. Superiority (Plato→Hobbes) and Relief (Spencer/Freud) captured the *suddenness*; incongruity theory (Beattie/Kant/Schopenhauer) relocated the cause to cognition; Suls/Shultz operationalized it as two-stage information processing; Raskin/Attardo formalized expectation as script activation and humor as script opposition; McGraw & Warren added the benign-appraisal constraint; Westbury et al. closed the loop by showing Shannon entropy quantitatively predicts funniness — grounding the whole tradition in the mathematics underlying LLM perplexity.
